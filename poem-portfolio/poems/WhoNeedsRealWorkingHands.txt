All my family worked with their hands.
Five digits firmly pressed into dirt.
Five siblings posed in cool prairie air.
All rusty joints rattle but, 
my joints squeak.

All my family’s hands are callused work.
Hands with scar tissue hold an
all too familiar shape for thick-stemmed shovels,

Now all that remains is a forgotten cellar. 
All those hand-me-down shoes with worn-out souls.
Five of you
all trying to get out.

You did.
And you met a man whose tissue
perfectly matched yours,
together hand-in-hand,
all you knew was callused work.
