A pine grove under a 
decadent pink sky. It is ours 
together. As we sprawl on a 
blanket checkered with 
my old secrets and yours,
My Summertime Strawberry. 

My love, you’d find a nook in the 
blanket’s folds, oblivious to the 
pixies dancing on your cheek. I’d run
my fingers across your neck 
and into your hair.
You’d smile and dream
my skin getting darker and my hair lighter.
Was it the sun or you? Did my heart beat pink or 
blue?

In the heat, I’d strip off my shirt.
You’d smirk and I’d toss it towards you, 
cotton draped over your head.
I’d feel the grass tickle my chest 
while we soaked in the 
warm rotisserie of the sun.

You’d complain about a table’s snarky words
until it didn’t matter. I’d sit attentive
because I loved to hear you talk. And 
I’d tell you how perfect your eyes looked until 
you’d absorb my pleasing words under 
our setting sun.

But, we never heard the evening doves wallow and
I’d never swim in your laugh.
You never gave us that chance.
To make it to the grove.
