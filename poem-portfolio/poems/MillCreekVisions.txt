I hold onto a dream:
A quiet pine grove where 
evening doves sing their glee. 
Under a decadent pink sky,
beside trees and birds, I lay
a checkered picnic blanket for you, 
my Summertime Strawberry. 

My love, you find a nook in the 
blanket’s folds, oblivious to the 
pixies dancing on your cheek. I run
my fingers across your neck 
and into your hair.
You smile and see
my skin getting darker and my hair glimmering.
Was it the sun or you? Did my heart beat pink or 
blue?

As grass prickles into my chest,
I dream
myself finally laying bare and
soaking up the warm rotisserie of the sun.
I dream 
of you tracing lines
down my back. Maps to places we’d never go.

We never heard the evening doves wallow and
I never swam in your laugh. So I wonder
was my dream alive for you too?
