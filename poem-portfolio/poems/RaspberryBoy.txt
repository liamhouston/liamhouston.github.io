Sweet boy, 

Son of dancing sparkles, son of the stars and moon, 
kill the man who says you aren’t enough. 

Don’t strain your neck at marbled idols. 
A sculptor shaped their sinew for your soft eyes. 
Because son, the world is greedy and 
sees how your sister gazes at beauty magazines. 
They know your tears glisten in the mirror, 
just like hers. But from you they want:
unbreakable, untameable, unrelenting grit,
shouldered, rippled, lean-fit,
meat-eater, wife-beater,
leader of the pack, statue that won’t crack,
MAN.

So, please son, when they tell you to suck it up—
spit it back. Don’t listen to broken boys 
who have fallen too deep. They’d rather drown
in quiet desperation. 
Son, rewrite the rules. Crack Poseidon’s statue, 
hide his shards in deep trenches. 
Replace your idols with rebellious love.

When they command you to fight—
let your feelings win. You are not a blanket 
for a cold corpse, you are a rich tapestry. 
Don’t let your heart be smaller than the bottle 
or the gun. Many men drink themselves flat,
crushing their dreams into crumpled paper balls,
and tossing them aside. You’ll become unfeeling
labour if you distort yourself for them. 
You can’t listen.

So, take a breath, pack a bag, and 
paint love on every wall you pass. 
Son, let fearless whispers fill your lover’s ears. 
Feel your youth like a raspberry on your tongue, 
play with its bumps and hairs, crush its juice
against the roof of your mouth
and slurp it down. 

My sweet boy, lose yourself in love.
