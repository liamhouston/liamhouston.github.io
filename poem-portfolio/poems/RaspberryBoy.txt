Sweet boy, 

Son of dancing sparkles, son of the stars and moon, 
kill the man who says you aren’t enough. 

Don’t strain your neck at marbled idols. A sculptor shaped their sinew just
for your soft eyes. Because son, the world is greedy and they learned you 
aren’t much different from your sister. They know how to make your tears
glisten in the mirror, just like hers. But from you they want:
unbreakable, untameable, unrelenting grit; shouldered, rippled, lean-fit
meat-eater, wife-beater; leader of the pack, statue that won’t crack
MAN.
When they command you to fight— let your feelings win.
Don’t listen to broken boys who have fallen too deep. You need to
escape the riptide before you help them. They’d rather drown in 
their quiet desperation. Son, rewrite the rules. Crack Poseidon’s statue
and hide his shards in deep trenches. Replace him with soulful love.
Soulful love is rebellion.
So, please son, when they tell you to suck it up— spit it back.
You are not a blanket for a cold corpse, you are a rich tapestry. Don’t
let your heart be smaller than the bottle or the gun. Many men drink
themselves flat, crushing their dreams into crumpled paper balls, and
tossing them in the fire of their true selves. But son, back away from 
the fire, you are not destined for unfeeling, disposable labour.

Let fearless whispers fill your lover’s ears. Paint it on every wall.
Feel your youth like a raspberry on your tongue, play with its 
bumps and hairs, crush its juice against the roof of your mouth
and slurp it down. 

My sweet boy, lose yourself in love.
